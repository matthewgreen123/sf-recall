The Road to Recall↓

  • May 22, 2018 School board Resolution No. 184-10A1: “In Support of a Formal Process in the Renaming of San Francisco Unified School District Schools,” is unanimously adopted by the school board. Written in the wake of the white supremacist rally in Charlottesville, the resolution calls for the renaming process to be led by a blue-ribbon panel selected by the superintendent.
  • Oct. 15, 2018 Mayor London Breed appoints Faauuga Moliga to the school board to fill a seat that opened when Hydra Mendoza-McDonnell resigned. Breed has since announced her support for recalling Moliga, as well as Collins and López, and will select their replacements if the effort to oust them is successful.
  • Nov. 6, 2018 Gabriela Lopez, Alison Collins and Faauuga Moliga elected to school board. The three, all people of color, have since focused much of their policymaking on the district’s historically underserved students.
  • June 25, 2019 The school board votes to paint over a controversial mural at Washington High School. The 1936 "Life of Washington" mural, by Victor Arnautoff, had been criticized for its questionable depiction of Native Americans and African Americans. The decision comes after a community advisory committee — consisting of local Native American community members, students, school representatives, district representatives, local artists and historians — recommends permanently removing the offensive content. But the board’s decision triggers a backlash from the school’s alumni association, art historians and local preservationists.
  • Aug. 13, 2019 The board reverses course on the Washington High mural, voting 4-3 to cover the painting rather than permanently destroy it. Board President Stevon Cook and board members Rachel Norton, Jenny Lam and Faauuga Moliga vote to insteae cover the mural. Commissioners Collins, Lopez and Mark Sanchez votee against it.
  • March 16, 2020 All SFUSD schools cancel in-person clases due to the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, and tranition to remote learning for undetermined amount of time.
  • Summer 2020 A parent group called Decreasing the Distance forms to start pushing schools to reopen. The group holds rallies to demand schools reopen and lobbies local and state elected officials. The group eventually evolves into the SF Parent Coalition, laying the organizational groundwork to propel the recall.
  • June 24, 2020 The school board unanimously passes resolution to limit police presence on campuses.
  • July 15, 2020 SFUSD announces, per Superintendent Vince Matthew’s recommendation, it will begin the 2020-21 school year in distance learning.
  • Aug. 28, 2020 The district shares an initial reopening plan. Under the plan, the city must meet certain public health indicators. Superintendent Matthews lays out district benchmarks, including a testing plan, staff training and labor agreements. Once met, SFUSD will apply to the city to reopen for priority student populations.
  • Sept. 24, 2020 The district shares an initial reopening plan. Under the plan, the city must meet certain public health indicators.   • Oct. 21, 2020 The school board approves a change to a lottery-based admission process at Lowell High School for the 2021-2022 school year only. Earlier in the month, Superintendent Matthews presented the policy to the board. With the switch to credit or no-credit grading for the spring semester and standardized testing paused because of the pandemic, district officials said the normal admissions process at Lowell would not be possible. The elite high school’s merit-based admissions system has been the subject of controversy for decades and anger over the board’s decision to do away with it helped galvanize some recall supporters.
